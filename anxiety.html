International Journal of Clinical & Experimental Hypnosis2019Meta-Analysis · 17 Trials

Hypnosis reduces anxiety more than 79% of control participants

A rigorous meta-analysis published in the International Journal of Clinical and Experimental Hypnosis analysed 17 randomised trials of hypnosis for anxiety. The study found a mean weighted effect size of 0.79 — meaning the average person receiving hypnosis improved more than 79% of those in control conditions. At follow-up, the effect size increased to 0.99, demonstrating lasting results.

"At the end of active treatment, 17 trials produced a mean weighted effect size of 0.79 (p ≤ .001), indicating the average participant receiving hypnosis reduced anxiety more than about 79% of control participants."

ScienceDirect · Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis202520 RCTs · 1,250 Patients

Hypnosis significantly reduces anxiety — p < 0.001

A comprehensive 2025 meta-analysis of 20 randomised controlled trials with 1,250 patients found hypnosis significantly reduced anxiety (SMD = −0.43, p < 0.001) compared to standard care. Physiological stress markers including heart rate and blood pressure were also reduced. The study concluded hypnosis is both effective and safe.

"Hypnosis significantly reduced anxiety (SMD = −0.43, 95% CI: −0.58 to −0.28, p < 0.001) compared to standard care. Physiological stress markers, including heart rate and blood pressure, were also reduced."

Hypnotherapy identified as "high programmatic priority" by the US National Institute of Health

A 20-year umbrella review published in Frontiers in Psychology found medium to large effect sizes for hypnosis on anxiety across multiple meta-analyses. Critically, the US National Center for Complementary and Integrative Health (NCCIH) designated hypnotherapy a treatment of "high programmatic priority" in 2021 — the first time since 2015 — supporting its evidence-based use for anxiety, chronic pain, PTSD and more.

"The NCCIH recognises evidence for the efficacy of hypnosis in the treatment of anxiety, IBS, chronic pain, PTSD, and hot flashes — and identified hypnotherapy as a treatment approach with 'high programmatic priority'."
