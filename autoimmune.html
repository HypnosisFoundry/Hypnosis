Hypnosis is effective in the control of autoimmune diseases

A comprehensive meta-analysis published in the peer-reviewed journal Neurological Sciences reviewed 51 separate studies and concluded that hypnosis is effective for autoimmune disease control. The review also noted that hypnotic suggestions improve patients' quality of life including physical symptoms and disease progression.

"A meta-analysis including 51 studies showed that hypnosis is effective in the control of autoimmune diseases... suggestions allow an improvement of the patients' quality of life including physical symptoms and disease progression."

Psychology & Health Journal 2000 Randomized Controlled Trial · 66 Patients

Hypnotherapy reduces joint pain, swelling and stiffness in Rheumatoid Arthritis

A rigorous randomized controlled trial of 66 patients with rheumatoid arthritis compared hypnotherapy against relaxation therapy and a control group. Participants receiving hypnotherapy used guided imagery to calm immune activity, practicing at least 3 times weekly. After 3 and 6 months, the hypnosis group showed greater improvements in joint pain, swelling and stiffness than both other groups — without changes in medication.

"Joint pain, joint swelling, and stiffness had improved more in the hypnosis group than in the control group, but also compared to the relaxation group. These improvements could not be attributed to a change in medication and therefore seem to stem from the hypnosis."

American Journal of Clinical Hypnosis 2007 · PubMed ID: 18030927 Systematic Review

The brain and immune system interact — hypnosis can therapeutically influence both

This systematic review of literature established the scientific foundation for using hypnosis in autoimmune treatment. It confirmed that the immune system interacts with the central nervous system with mutual influence, and provided a rational basis for hypnotherapy. Five clinical case studies across different autoimmune disorders were documented with instructions for clinicians.

"Significant scientific advances have confirmed that the immune system does interact with the central nervous system with mutual influence. This provides a rational foundation for the role of using hypnosis and imagery to therapeutically influence the immune system."

Psychoneuroimmunology Research 2003 · PubMed ID: 12570090 Clinical Study · Immune Biomarkers

Hypnosis produces measurable changes in immune T-cells and cortisol

This study tested whether hypnosis could measurably alter the T-cell subsets most relevant to autoimmune activity. Blood samples were drawn at five time points before and after hypnotic intervention. Results showed statistically significant reductions in immune inflammatory markers — providing biological evidence for the mechanism behind hypnotherapy's effects.

"Following hypnosis intervention, statistically significant immunological effects were noted. The proportion of T-cells expressing IFN-gamma decreased (p = .0001) and IL-2 also decreased (p = .013) — both key inflammatory markers in autoimmune disease."
→ View on PubMed
Pain Medicine Journal · PMC 2012 Randomized Controlled Pilot Study

Hypnosis may reduce pro-inflammatory cytokine activity linked to autoimmune flares

A randomized controlled pilot study examined whether hypnosis directly influences the HPA axis and inflammatory cytokine activity — the same biological pathways that trigger autoimmune flares. Results showed medium-to-large effect sizes for cortisol and TNF-alpha reduction, supporting the need for larger trials in conditions like rheumatoid arthritis.

"If confirmed that hypnosis reliably induces a decrease in HPA axis and pro-inflammatory cytokine activity, this modality may potentially be used as an adjuvant in clinical therapy involving autoimmune conditions where inflammation is a primary driver."
→ View on PMC / NIH
ScienceDirect 2000 Clinical Review · Crohn's Disease

Hypnotherapy associated with remission in Crohn's disease cases

This review examined hypnotherapy as a complementary approach to Crohn's disease — a chronic inflammatory bowel condition with autoimmune involvement. Recent clinical case studies and reports showed that different forms of hypnotherapy resulted in measurable improvements in disease activity and, in some cases, full remission — while also demonstrating modulation of immune function parameters.

"Recent clinical case studies and anecdotal reports have shown that the use of different forms of hypnotherapy for the treatment of Crohn's have actually resulted in cures... while providing evidence of the modulation of immune function parameters by hypnosis."
